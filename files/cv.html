About Me

I am a graduate software engineer @ Net-A-Porter Group, working with tech teams at mrporter.com and net-a-porter.com Having started in September 2014, I have tried my hand in automated testing, UX design and full stack web development, and over the next 12 months I will join engineers at IOS and back-end teams. Despite being at the start of my career, I commit to the code base on the daily basis: building responsive layouts, writing automated and unit tests and implementing new features alongside some of the best developers in the industry.

Education

I hold a First Class Honours Degree in Computer Science from the University of Surrey. For my final year project I developed a Google Glass application, which was a karaoke platform-featuring nursery rhymes. The project was awarded “ai Corporation Prize for best use of Innovative Web Technologies in a final year project” prize. I was the first student and one of the first few engineers in the UK to write a Glassware application ahead of Google Glass becoming available in the UK.

Interests

In addition to software engineering I like to explore children-tech interactions and relationships. I have organised and led a Code Club at my local primary school, where I taught basics of programming and web development to a class of 21 children weekly for a year. Currently being in full time employment and a hands-on mother of two, I am unable to commit to a permanent role as a volunteer, but I continue to teach programming to my 8-year old son. We upload his creations to this Scratch page. Have a look and let me know what you think!
